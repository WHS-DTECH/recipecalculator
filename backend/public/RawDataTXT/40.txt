JSON-LD Recipe Instructions:
Tear the hot cross buns into small pieces and place in a bowl. Heat the olive oil or butter in a pan over medium heat.
Add the onion and cook for 5 to 6 minutes until soft and sweet. Add the garlic and rosemary and cook for another 30 seconds.
Tip the onion mixture over the hot cross buns, add the parsley, then season lightly with salt and pepper. Toss gently to combine. Set aside to cool slightly.
Lay the lamb out flat on a board, skin side down. If it is uneven in thickness, gently open it out so it is as even as possible.
In a small bowl, mix the olive oil, lemon zest, garlic, rosemary, salt and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the lamb.
Spoon the stuffing evenly over the lamb, leaving a small border around the edges. Roll the lamb up tightly from the long edge, enclosing the stuffing.
Tie securely with kitchen string at 4 to 5 cm intervals so it holds its shape.
If you have time, cover and rest the lamb in the fridge for 1 hour to help the flavours settle.
Preheat the oven to 190°C fan bake. Place the lamb on a rack set over a roasting dish. Roast for 1 hour to 1 hour 15 minutes for medium, or until a thermometer reads around 60°C in the thickest part.
Remove from the oven, loosely cover with foil and rest for at least 15 minutes.
Slice thickly and serve with roast kūmara, seasonal greens and plenty of gravy or pan juices.

Visible Page Text:
Easter Lamb with hot cross bun stuffing
A showstopping Easter lamb roast with a hot cross bun twist

Serves

8 - 10

Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

1 hr 15 mins

Easter and lamb go hand in hand in New Zealand, and this boneless lamb roast is a fresh take on a much-loved tradition. Tender New Zealand lamb is rolled and stuffed with toasted hot cross buns, fragrant rosemary, garlic and bright lemon zest, creating a roast that feels familiar yet quietly impressive.

The hot cross bun stuffing adds gentle spice and richness, soaking up the lamb juices as it roasts, while rosemary and lemon keep everything savoury and balanced. Boneless and easy to carve, this Easter lamb roast is ideal for long lunches, relaxed entertaining and sharing with family and friends.

Whether you are hosting Easter Sunday or planning a special autumn roast, this recipe delivers a juicy, flavour packed centrepiece that celebrates the best of New Zealand lamb and the flavours of the season.

Kirsten McCaffrey

Method
1

Tear the hot cross buns into small pieces and place in a bowl. Heat the olive oil or butter in a pan over medium heat.

2

Add the onion and cook for 5 to 6 minutes until soft and sweet. Add the garlic and rosemary and cook for another 30 seconds.

3

Tip the onion mixture over the hot cross buns, add the parsley, then season lightly with salt and pepper. Toss gently to combine. Set aside to cool slightly.

4

Lay the lamb out flat on a board, skin side down. If it is uneven in thickness, gently open it out so it is as even as possible.

5

In a small bowl, mix the olive oil, lemon zest, garlic, rosemary, salt and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the lamb.

6

Spoon the stuffing evenly over the lamb, leaving a small border around the edges. Roll the lamb up tightly from the long edge, enclosing the stuffing.

7

Tie securely with kitchen string at 4 to 5 cm intervals so it holds its shape.

8

If you have time, cover and rest the lamb in the fridge for 1 hour to help the flavours settle.

9

Preheat the oven to 190°C fan bake. Place the lamb on a rack set over a roasting dish. Roast for 1 hour to 1 hour 15 minutes for medium, or until a thermometer reads around 60°C in the thickest part.

10

Remove from the oven, loosely cover with foil and rest for at least 15 minutes.

11

Slice thickly and serve with roast kūmara, seasonal greens and plenty of gravy or pan juices.

Pro tips:

The gentle sweetness and spice from the hot cross buns pairs beautifully with lamb, but it should stay savoury. If your buns are very sweet, use slightly less and balance with extra rosemary and lemon zest.
